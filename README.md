# Are College Sports Driving Tuition?

### Data Analysis Project – University of Rochester

This project examines a common claim that college athletics are a major driver of rising university tuition. Using financial data from the University of Rochester, the analysis evaluates athletics spending trends, its share of university expenditures, and its cost per student.

## Key Findings

• Athletics spending increased from about $8 million in 2004 to around $14 million in 2023.

• However, athletics account for less than **1% of total university spending** (excluding hospital spending).

• When distributed across students, athletics cost roughly **$1300–$1900 per student per year**.

• Eliminating athletics would therefore reduce tuition only marginally.

The analysis suggests that athletics are often treated as a scapegoat for rising tuition, while structural factors such as administrative expansion and tuition pricing strategies may play a larger role.

## Data Sources

• Equity in Athletics Disclosure Act (EADA)  
• Integrated Postsecondary Education Data System (IPEDS)  
• U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics CPI data
